Types of Rocket Engines


( Image source: Nasa, Liquid Rocket Engine )

Liquid Fuel Engines

This is how a cross section of a liquid fuel engine looks like. It consists of 3 parts.- Liquid Fuel, Liquid Oxidizer and Combustion Chamber.

The liquid fuel is the propellent.

Liquid oxidizer is used so that the engine can be used in space or any place with low or no oxygen (as combustion requires oxygen)

The pumps pump the fuel and oxidizers in a pre-determined ratio to maximize combustion and force release.

Lastly the combustion chamber is where the propellent exits the rocket and pushes the air downward, causing the rocket to be propelled up.


( Image source: Nasa, Solid Rocket Engine )

Solid Fuel Engine

The solid fuel takes up most of the rocket as it the propellent. It consists of a mixture of solid fuel and oxidizer in a solid form.

The ignition motor, as the name suggest is a motor that ignites the rocket, allowing the propellent to burn.

Lastly the combustion chamber is where the propellent exits the rocket and pushes the air downward, causing the rocket to be propelled up.


( Image source: Sasi Kiran Palateerdham, Hybrid Rocket Engine Diagram )

Hybrid Engines

Researchers are currently working on a hybrid engine which will essentially be a solid fuel engine but one in which you can control the thrust percentage.

Here's a cross-section of how it would look like. It has similar design as the solid fuel engine, however, instead of a mixture of a mixture of solid fuel and oxidizer in a solid form,it is only going to be propellent.

The oxidizer will be stored above the propellent and will be attached to pump, which will allow only a limited amount of oxidizer to pass though. Using this, we will be able to control the thrust percentage of the rocket
