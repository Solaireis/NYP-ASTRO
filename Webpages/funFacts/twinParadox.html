This is the simplified explanation to the Twin Paradox. In short, time is relative to the observer, in special relativity the faster one travels the slower time will appear to be to a static observer. This can be proven using the Lorentz Factor, at 86.6% of the speed of light, the lorentz factor is 2. Therefore time will be slower in space by twice the time than it is on Earth.


Therefore if we were to have a experiement where 1 twin is on Earth and another twin travels on a spaceship and back and forth in 10 light years, the twin on earth will be about 11 years older than the twin who went abroad the spaceship.
