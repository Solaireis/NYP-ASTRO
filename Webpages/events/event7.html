First and foremost, we will introduced the definition of an exoplanet

Exoplanet Definition

Planets that orbit around other stars are called exoplanets.

Some are Super Earth

Orbits around a M-type star

Takes 11.2 days to complete one orbit

4 light years away

Detecting Exoplanets

Radial Velocity

Transit

Direct imaging

Gravitational microlensing

Astrometry

Radial Velocity

Watching for the “wobbly”

The method used is one called 'Doppler shift’

The waves can either be wider or more closely packed depending on the movement of object, because the object emits energy

Transit

Searching for the shadows

When a planet passes directly between an observer and the star it orbits, it blocks some of that star's light.

Direct Imaging

Taking Pictures

Coronography

Star Shade

Gravitational Microlensing

Light in a gravity lens

Gravitational microlensing happens when a star or planet's gravity focuses the light of another, more distant star, in a way that makes it temporarily seem brighter.

Astrometry

Minuscule movements

Astronomers take a bunch of images of a star and other stars around it.
